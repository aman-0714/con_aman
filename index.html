It is my great pleasure to welcome you to the International Conference on AI Engineering and Innovation (AIEI 2026), scheduled to be held on March 26–28, 2026 at the Department of Electrical Engineering, National Institute of Technology Jamshedpur, India . This inaugural edition marks a significant step toward advancing Artificial Intelligence and Engineering Innovations.

AIEI 2026 has been envisioned as a dynamic global platform for researchers, academicians, industry professionals, and innovators to present original contributions and explore the latest in AI-driven systems and applications, with a strong emphasis on international collaboration.

This event is jointly organized by NIT Jamshedpur, India and USD Artificial Intelligence Research, USA , in collaboration with IEEE and associated bodies.

The conference features keynote sessions, technical paper presentations, tutorials, industry sessions, and panel discussions across various AI domains. All registered and presented papers will be submitted to IEEE Xplore Digital Library subject to eligibility criteria.

About NIT Jamshedpur

Dr B R Ambedkar National Institute of Technology was established in the year 1987 as Regional Engineering College and was given the status of National Institute of Technology (Deemed University) by the Government of India on October 17, 2002 under the aegis of Ministry of Education (Shiksha Mantralaya), New Delhi. Now the Ministry of Education (Shiksha Mantralaya), Government of India has declared the Institute as “Institute of National Importance” under the act of Parliament-2007. A large number of reputed Industrial houses in the country visit the Institution and select the final year students as Engineers/ Management Trainees.

Department of Electrical Engineering

The Department of Electrical Engineering was established in 1960 and has consistently produced industry-ready engineers.

The department is actively involved in research and development across emerging areas of electrical engineering.

It offers postgraduate programs in Power Systems and Power Electronics & Drives along with Ph.D programs.
